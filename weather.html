Weather

On this page you can retrieve weather data for some of the biggest penguin hotspots. Monitoring weather data like this is important as the main reason for so many of the penguin species being classed as endangered or vulnerable is climate change.

Climate change means that the ice so many penguins call home is melting, this not only impacts their homes and breeding colonies but also means a reduction in the population of krill, which is a large part of the penguin diet. In Antarctica, climate change is causing unprecedented rain or melted snowfall and ice, creating puddles on the ground which can cause hypothermia for chicks of penguins who lay their eggs on the ground as these chicks have not yet developed their waterproof feathers.
